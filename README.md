# Global-Economic-and-Social-Trends-Analysis

## Overview
This project explores **global socioeconomic patterns** using the World Development Indicators (WDI) dataset.  
Through a series of statistical and interactive visualizations, it examines the relationships between **GDP per capita, life expectancy, population density, birth/death rates, and infant mortality**, while highlighting regional disparities and global trends over time.

## Objectives
Through a combination of statistical plots, comparative charts, and interactive visualizations, the project seeks to:

1. Highlight Economic Distribution and Growth

2. Explore Health and Demographic Outcomes

3. Uncover Relationships Between Key Indicators

4. Compare Regional and Income Group Disparities

## Dataset
- **Source:** https://mavenanalytics.io/data-playground/world-economic-indicators - World Bank
- **Format:** CSV file
- **Coverage:** 2000–2017

## 🛠️ Tools
- Python
- Pandas  
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn  
- Plotly Express

## Key Insights
- **Wealth & Health:** Higher GDP per capita strongly predicts longer life expectancy and lower infant mortality.  
- **Population Density:** Adds context but shows weak correlation with wealth and health outcomes.  
- **Regional Gaps:** North America & Europe lead; Sub Saharan Africa & South Asia lag; middle income regions show steady progress.  
- **Demographic Transition:** Low income countries face high birth/death rates; high income countries show stable or aging populations.  
- **Global Trends:** GDP per capita rose steadily from 2000 to mid 2010s, then stabilized, with East Asia’s role growing significantly.
